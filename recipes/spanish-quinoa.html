Spanish Quinoa

While we know the Spanish make amazing rice (see paella), rice is normally really high in carbs and low in fiber. Instead we are going to make this fantastic dish with a different "grain" called quinoa! Native to Peru, it is a complete protein and has a solid carb-fiber ratio to reduce the carb-load of this dish!

For the Beans

  • 2 cans of Pinto Beans
  • 1 can of Diced Tomatoes
  • 1 jar of Salsa (Pick one with a spice to it!)
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 1 tablespoon of Diced Garlic
  • 2 tablespoons of Canola Oil (or nuetral oil)
  • 2 tablespoons of Chili Powder
  • 1 tablespoons of Cumin
  • 1 tablespoons of Creole Seasoning (with salt)

For the Quinoa

  • 1.5 cups of Quinoa
  • 3 cups of water

Steps

  1. Start the quinoa first because it will need to be added to this dish after the veggies are cooked. Add the water and quinoa to a pot and put it on high until it boils. After reduce to medium or lower to simmer.
  2. In a separate pot cook the onions, garlic, and bell peppers in the oil with chili powder, cumin, and creole seasoning. Once the onions are translucent, add the beans, tomatoes, salsa, and quinoa and reduce.
